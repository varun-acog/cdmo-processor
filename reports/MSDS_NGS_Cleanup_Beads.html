1. Introduction

1.1 Overview

Core Function: NEXTflex™ NGS Cleanup Beads are primarily used in genomics research for purifying nucleic acids during next-generation sequencing workflows.

Commercialization Date: The beads were first commercialized in the early 2020s as part of an increase in demand for high-throughput sequencing technologies.

1.1.1 Background & Market Context

These beads are heavily utilized in genomics and microbiome research sectors. The global NGS market is estimated to be worth several billion dollars, driven by advances in personalized medicine, cancer research, and molecular diagnostics.

1.2 Key Physical and Chemical Properties

Appearance: A clear liquid with a brown precipitate and odorless quality.

Hazards: While sodium azide is present in low concentrations, it can form explosive compounds with heavy metals. The product is not classified as hazardous under EC 1272/2008 (CLP/GHS).

1.2.1 Physical Appearance and Basic Characteristics

The beads must be stored away from strong acids, bases, and oxidizers. Volatility is not a major handling concern due to their stable nature under recommended storage conditions.

1.2.2 Hazardous Nature and Safety Classifications

According to EC 1272/2008 (CLP/GHS), no hazardous classification is required. It is recommended to wear PPE such as safety glasses, chemical goggles, and nitrile gloves.

Genomics, biotechnology, and molecular diagnostics

Main factors influencing growth include advancements in sequencing technologies, increasing applications in personalized medicine, and a rising interest in microbiome studies.
